## 3. Addendum

What are the conditions for your residence and work permit?

Your residence and work permit is granted with a view to a temporary stay under section 9 a (2) (ix) of the Danish Aliens Consolidation Act and section 25 (1) of the Executive Order on Aliens' Access to Denmark.

In order to maintain your residence and work permit, you must meet a number of conditions at all times. If you fail to do so, you can lose your permission to reside and work in Denmark.

**Your residence and work permit is conditional upon the following:**

1. **Your terms of employment must continuously be equivalent to Danish standards.**

1.1. Your job, job description and employment terms correspond with the information in your application and may not change significantly.

1.2. The salary you receive must correspond, at a minimum, to the salary stated in your employment contract of 15 January 2026.

1.3. Your working hours must be 37 hours per week in accordance with your contract.

1.4. Your working hours must be scheduled in accordance with the collective agreement in question. This means that your average working hours over a seven-day period, calculated over a period of 12 months, must not exceed 48 hours including overtime. Periods of paid annual leave and periods of sick leave shall not be included or shall be neutral in the calculation of the average.

1.5. You do not change employer, please cf. below under the section "What if your situation changes?".

1.6. You must continue to have a Danish bank account in your own name.

1.7. Your salary must continue to be paid into your Danish bank account.

1.8. Your employer must give you the right to take holiday annually at a minimum in accordance with the terms of your contract.

1.9. In case your employment is terminated, your notice period must correspond to the terms of notice in your contract.

1.10. An accompanying family member to you must not have had his/her residence permit revoked more than once due to lack of a required work permit.

1.11. You or your accompanying family members, if any, must not receive support under the Act on Active Social Policy, e.g. social security, start help benefits or rehabilitation benefits.

1.12. You must always be in possession of a valid passport or another travel document accepted by the Danish authorities.

## 3.1. When can you apply for an extension of your residence and work permit?

You can apply for an extension of your residence and work permit at the earliest 3 months before your residence and work permit expires and at the latest on the last valid day of the residence and work permit.

In order to be granted an extension of your residence and work permit you must as a general rule continuously have met and still meet the conditions for your residence and work permit.

**Please note:** It is a condition for an extension of the residence and work permit that you have a valid passport. We can only grant you a residence and work permit valid until 3 months before the expiry date of your passport.

If you apply for an extension of this residence and work permit more than 3 months before your residence and work permit expires, your application will be rejected without processing, and you must apply again at the earliest 3 months before your residence and work permit expires.

If you submit the application in Denmark after your residence and work permit has expired, your application will likely be rejected.

## 3.2. How do you prove that you hold a Danish residence and work permit?

You will receive a residence card, which proves that you hold a Danish residence permit for as long as the residence card is valid. Please notice, that your residence card can have a longer duration than your work permit.

During your residence in Denmark, you must always have your residence card with you.

Until you receive your residence card this letter is proof of your residence and work permit. We recommend that you keep this letter with you until you receive your residence card.

Other documents or cards - for example a Danish health insurance card (sundhedskort or yellow card) - cannot be used as proof of your residence and work permit.

## 3.3. When will you receive your residence card?

We have ordered your residence card in connection with this decision. The card will be sent to your registered Danish address within 4 to 6 weeks from the date of this decision.

Remember to write your full name on your letterbox. Otherwise, your residence card will not be delivered.

If you do not receive your card within 8 weeks, please contact us.

## 4. Arrival and residence in Denmark

**Have you been registered in the Entry/Exit System (EES)?** We are currently implementing the EES across the external Schengen borders. EES is an IT system for registering third country nationals each time they cross an external Schengen Area border, if they travel on a visa (not a residence card) or a visa free stay. You can read more about EES here: EES European Union (europa.eu).

During the implementation period, some travellers will be registered in EES, and some will not. This means, that an entry into the Schengen Area may be registered in a traveller file in the EES. Whether or not an entry has been registered does not affect the residence permit. Please note, that passports will still be stamped during the implementation period.

**What should you do now or upon entering Denmark?**

### 4.1. Register your Danish address

You must register your Danish address no later than 5 days after you have moved into your accommodation in Denmark. You do this by visiting your local municipality (kommune) or one of the International Citizen Service centres (ICS). You can find contact information at lifeindenmark.dk. Remember to bring this letter, your passport, your contact information and/or other proof of your address in Denmark.

### 4.2. Open a Danish bank account in your own name

You must continue to have a Danish bank account in your own name. Your salary must continue to be paid into a Danish bank account in your own name. You can lose your permission to work and reside in Denmark if you fail to comply with these two conditions. Since Danish banks are private companies, we cannot recommend one or the other. You must find a bank of your own choosing. Documentation requirements in order to open a Danish bank account may vary depending on the bank.

### 4.3. Find out if you are entitled to free Danish language lessons

You are subject to some of the regulations in the Danish Integration Act. Among other things, this means that you may be entitled to free Danish language education. For further information, please contact your local municipality (kommune).

## 5. During your residence in Denmark

**Can you take up sideline employment (second job)?** Your residence and work permit only allows you to work with the specified employer mentioned in the first part of this letter. However, you can apply for an additional work permit for sideline employment on our website. Your sideline employment must be naturally related to the job that forms the basis of your residence and work permit. Be aware, that you are not allowed to start working in your sideline employment before you have been granted a permit for it.

**Can you carry out voluntary work?** Your residence and work permit allows you to carry out voluntary, unpaid work as long as the permit is valid. The voluntary, unpaid work must not replace paid employees and it is therefore a condition that the work in question is normally carried out unpaid and voluntarily.

The voluntary, unpaid work must be carried out for a formally organized organization or association and must have a genuine non-profit purpose, which must appear in the rules governing the organization and in the organizations actual work. Voluntary, unpaid work therefore cannot be carried out, if the event has a commercial or partly commercial aim, e.g. voluntary, unpaid work for a larger sporting event.

You can read more about the conditions for unpaid voluntary work on newtodenmark.dk/voluntary-work.

**Can you lose your permission to reside in Denmark?** You can lose your permission to reside in Denmark for a number of reasons, for example (not exhaustive):

1. If you fail to comply with the conditions for your residence and work permit.

1.1. If the basis for granting your residence and work permit was incorrect or no longer exists.

1.2. If you do not start working within the time limit, which is described in the first part of this letter.

1.3. If you give up your residence in Denmark or if you stay outside Denmark for 6 consecutive months or more, your residence and work permit will automatically lapse. A stay in Greenland or the Faroe Islands is regarded as a stay outside Denmark. You must apply for and be granted a dispensation in advance/before leaving Denmark to prevent your permit from lapsing in these situations.

**What if your situation changes?** It may be necessary to apply for a new residence and work permit if your situation changes. This may be the case:

1. If you lose your job through no fault of your own, e.g. due to cut-backs. In this situation, you must inform us immediately.

1.1. If you get a new job with a new employer. In this situation, you must, as a general rule, apply for and be granted a new residence and work permit based on the new job before you can start working at your new job. However, in some situations you can start working as soon as you have submitted the application (the job change rule).

Find out if this applies to your situation on newtodenmark.dk/jobchange. Read more about what to do if your situation changes on newtodenmark.dk.

**Your case may be selected for an immigration check** We regularly select cases for an immigration check in order to make sure that the conditions of the residence and work permit are still met. Your case may be selected for an immigration check even though there are no specific indications that you do not meet the conditions.

Immigration checks may be performed in the following ways:

1. Register checks, e.g. in the Civil Registration System (CPR).

1.1. Comparison of information in our registers and those of the Danish Immigration Service with information from the CPR, the Building and Housing register (BBR), the Central Business Register (CVR) and the E-income register.

1.2. Enquiries with other authorities, e.g. local authorities.

1.3. Enquiries with third parties, e.g. your employer or place of study.

1.4. Personal enquiries at your place of residence, study or work.

If the results of an immigration check indicate that you do not meet the conditions for your residence and work permit, you may lose your permit.

**What documentation do you need to travel abroad during your residence in Denmark?** You must always carry a valid passport or another travel document accepted by the Danish authorities when leaving and returning to Denmark while your residence permit is valid.

Furthermore:

- If the entry visa in your passport is still valid on the day you want to re-enter Denmark: You can leave and return to Denmark.

- If the entry visa in your passport has expired, and you have not received your residence card: You must apply for and be granted a re-entry permit before you leave Denmark. Find more information about re-entry permits on newtodenmark.dk/Re-entry-permit.

- If you have received your residence card: You can leave and return to Denmark as long as you also carry your valid residence card.

You cannot use this letter as a travel document. It is your own responsibility to comply with the entry and exit rules of all countries you need to transit through before re-entering Denmark.

**How do you apply for another residence permit while in Denmark?** If you want to apply for a residence permit on another basis while in Denmark, e.g. employment or as an accompanying family member, you must submit your application before your current residence and work permit, visa or visa free period expires.

If you submit an application in Denmark after your current residence and work permit, visa or visa free period has expired, your application will likely be rejected as you are residing illegally in Denmark. You risk being reported to the police, fined, expelled, deported from Denmark and given an entry ban.

## 6. Leaving Denmark and the Schengen Area at the end of your residence

**When do you have to leave Denmark and the Schengen Area after your residence and work permit expires?** You are allowed to reside in Denmark until your residence and work permit expires. You must leave Denmark and the Schengen Area no later than on the expiry date of your residence and work permit, unless you are allowed to stay on other grounds. If you have a pending application for a residence permit with the Danish Immigration Service, please contact them to find out if you are allowed to stay in Denmark while they process your application.

If you are not allowed to stay on other grounds and you do not leave Denmark and the Schengen Area within the deadline for your departure, you are residing illegally in Denmark and the Schengen Area. You risk being reported to the police, fined, expelled, deported from Denmark and given an entry ban.

**How do you apply for another residence permit after your departure from Denmark?** If you want to apply for another residence permit in Denmark after your departure from Denmark, you can submit your application from your home country, or from a country in which you have resided legally for at least 3 months prior to the application.

## 7. Applicable law

The legislation on foreign nationals' entry and residence in Denmark is in Danish and publicly available on retsinformation.dk. We also link to some of this legislation in Danish on newtodenmark.dk/legislation.

For the purpose of general guidance only we have translated some of the content of the rules relevant to this decision in the following text. The text is not an authorized legal translation.

The rules relevant to this decision are:

The Danish Aliens Consolidation Act no. 1183 of 25 September 2025 as subsequently amended

- **Section 9 a (2) (ix):** A residence permit may be given to a foreign national who has been offered an employment within the agricultural sector as a herdsman.

- **Section 9 a (4) sentence:** You must not receive benefits granted under the terms of the Active Social Policy Act.

- **Section 9 a (4) sentence:** Your accompanying family members, if any, must not have had their residence permit revoked more than once due to lack of a required work permit.

- **Section 9 a (25):** Your salary must be paid into a Danish bank account.

- **Section 19 (1)(i) and (iii):** A residence and work permit may be revoked if the basis of the residence and work permit is no longer present or the conditions for the permit is not met.

- **Section 39:** A foreign national must upon entry, while staying in Denmark and upon exit be in possession of a valid passport or another document acceptable as a travel document.

- **Section 40:** A foreign national must provide the information necessary for assessing whether a residence and work permit can be granted, revoked or cancelled or whether the foreign national is staying or working legally in Denmark.

The Executive Order on Aliens Access to Denmark no. 1354 of 17 November 2025

- **Section 17 (1):** Residence and work permits under section 9 a of the Danish Aliens Consolidation Act are granted with a validity until 3 months before the expiry date of the applicant's travel document.

- **Section 25 (1):** A work permit is granted for a foreign national with a residence permit under section 9 a of the Danish Aliens Consolidation Act.

- **Section 26 (1):** A work permit is granted for a job with a specific employer.
