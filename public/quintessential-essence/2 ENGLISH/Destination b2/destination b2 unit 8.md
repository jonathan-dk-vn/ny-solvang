# UNIT 8: THE PASSIVE & CAUSATIVE - "NGHỆ THUẬT LẬT NGƯỢC"

### 🎯 Tại sao cần học Passive Voice?

So sánh hai câu: "Someone stole my car" vs "My car was stolen". Câu nào nghe chuyên nghiệp hơn? Passive Voice không chỉ là "đảo ngược" câu - nó là nghệ thuật tạo ra sự trang trọng, khách quan và tập trung vào điều quan trọng!

---

## 8.1. THE PASSIVE - THỂ BỊ ĐỘNG

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Khi nào "người làm" không quan trọng bằng "việc được làm"?

**Bản chất:** Passive Voice chuyển trọng tâm từ **người thực hiện** sang **hành động** hoặc **kết quả**.

### 📝 CÔNG THỨC CHUNG:
**Noun + be (chia thì) + Past Participle (V3) + (by/with + noun)**

### 📊 BẢNG CHUYỂN ĐỔI CÁC THÌ:

| Thì | Active | Passive |
|-----|--------|---------|
| **Present Simple** | They grow bananas. | Bananas **are grown**. |
| **Present Continuous** | They are decorating the café. | The café **is being decorated**. |
| **Present Perfect** | Has anyone peeled the carrots? | **Have** the carrots **been peeled**? |
| **Past Simple** | They served the meal. | The meal **was served**. |
| **Past Continuous** | They were preparing the bill. | The bill **was being prepared**. |
| **Past Perfect** | Someone had eaten all food. | All food **had been eaten**. |
| **Future (will)** | We will deliver your pizza. | Your pizza **will be delivered**. |
| **Future (be going to)** | They are going to sue Burgerland. | Burgerland **is going to be sued**. |
| **Modal** | You should brush the chicken. | The chicken **should be brushed**. |

### 🎯 KHI NÀO DÙNG PASSIVE:

#### 1. Không biết ai làm:
- *My groceries **have been stolen**!*

#### 2. Hiển nhiên ai làm:
- *A boy **was arrested** yesterday.* (hiển nhiên là cảnh sát bắt)

#### 3. Không quan trọng ai làm:
- *The French bistro **is being knocked down**.* (quan trọng là việc phá dỡ)

#### 4. Muốn nhấn mạnh/trang trọng:
- *The potato **was brought** to Europe by Sir Walter Raleigh.*

### 🔥 BY vs WITH:

| Giới từ | Cách dùng | Ví dụ |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **BY** | Người/Tác nhân thực hiện | *painted **by** Picasso* |
| **WITH** | Công cụ/Phương tiện | *cut **with** a knife* |

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Lạm dụng "by":**
   - ❌ *This steak was overcooked by someone.*
   - ✅ *This steak was overcooked.*

2. **Dùng passive với động từ không phù hợp:**
   - ❌ *The accident was happened yesterday.*
   - ✅ *The accident happened yesterday.*

3. **Quên "being" trong continuous passive:**
   - ❌ *The house is built.* (khi muốn nói đang xây)
   - ✅ *The house is being built.*

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **Active:** Chủ ngữ **làm** hành động
- **Passive:** Chủ ngữ **nhận** hành động
- **By** = người làm, **With** = dụng cụ

## 8.2. IMPERSONAL PASSIVE - THỂ BỊ ĐỘNG KHÔNG NGÔI

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Làm sao nói "Người ta nói rằng..." một cách trang trọng?

**Bản chất:** Cách diễn đạt **ý kiến của nhiều người** một cách **khách quan, trang trọng**.

### 📝 HAI CẤU TRÚC:

#### Cấu trúc 1: It + be + said/believed/thought + that clause
- *It **is thought that** he is a great chef.*

#### Cấu trúc 2: Subject + be + said/believed/thought + to infinitive
- *He **is thought to be** a great chef.*

### 🎯 VÍ DỤ CHI TIẾT:

| Active | Passive (Cấu trúc 1) | Passive (Cấu trúc 2) |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| People think he is great. | **It is thought that** he is great. | He **is thought to be** great. |
| People believe he was great. | **It is believed that** he was great. | He **is believed to have been** great. |
| People say he has influenced others. | **It is said that** he has influenced others. | He **is said to have influenced** others. |

### 🔥 CÁC ĐỘNG TỪ THƯỜNG DÙNG:
*say, believe, think, claim, estimate, report, consider, suppose, expect, know*

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **Cấu trúc 1:** Khách quan hơn (*It is said that...*)
- **Cấu trúc 2:** Tập trung vào chủ thể (*He is said to...*)

## 8.3. THE CAUSATIVE - THỂ SƯ KHIẾN

### 🤔 Mở đầu: "I cut my hair" vs "I had my hair cut" - Ai là người cắt?

**Bản chất:** Diễn tả việc **nhờ người khác làm** thay vì tự làm.

### 📝 CÔNG THỨC:
**have/get + object + past participle**

### 🎯 CÁC CÁCH DÙNG:

#### 1. Sắp xếp cho ai đó làm:
- *I **have** my groceries **delivered** weekly.* (nhờ siêu thị giao hàng)
- *We **are having** a new cooker **put in** tomorrow.* (nhờ thợ lắp bếp)

#### 2. Tình huống không mong muốn:
- *We **had** our garden **vandalized**.* (khu vườn bị phá hoại)
- *They **have had** their microwave **stolen**.* (lò vi sóng bị trộm)

### 🔄 HAVE vs GET:

| Have | Get |
|------|-----|
| Trang trọng hơn | Thân thiện hơn |
| *have it repaired* | *get it repaired* |

### 🔥 CẤU TRÚC MỞ RỘNG:

#### Get/Have + somebody + to do:
- *Get the chef **to prepare** a vegetarian meal.*

#### Have + somebody + do (không "to"):
- *Have the chef **prepare** a vegetarian meal.*

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Nhầm lẫn "làm tự tay" vs "nhờ làm":**
   - *I cut my hair.* (tự cắt)
   - *I had my hair cut.* (nhờ thợ cắt)

2. **Quên past participle:**
   - ❌ *I had my car wash.*
   - ✅ *I had my car washed.*

## 8.4. DIRECT & INDIRECT OBJECTS - TÂN NGỮ TRỰC TIẾP & GIÁN TIẾP

### 🤔 Mở đầu: "I gave John a book" có thể passive theo mấy cách?

**Bản chất:** Một số động từ có **hai tân ngữ** → có **hai cách** chuyển passive!

### 📝 NHỮNG ĐỘNG TỪ HAI TÂN NGỮ:
*bring, buy, get, give, lend, make, offer, owe, pass, promise, send, show, take, teach, tell, write*

### 🎯 VÍ DỤ CHUYỂN ĐỔI:

#### Active (2 cách viết):
- *A friend gave **my sister** this book.* (indirect + direct)
- *A friend gave this book **to my sister**.* (direct + to + indirect)

#### Passive (2 cách):
- ***My sister** was given this book.* (indirect object làm chủ ngữ)
- ***This book** was given to my sister.* (direct object làm chủ ngữ)

### 🔄 GIỚI TỪ THƯỜNG DÙNG:

| Động từ | Giới từ | Ví dụ |
|---------|---------|-------|
| give, send, show | **to** | *given to Mary* |
| buy, make, cook | **for** | *made for the children* |

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **Tân ngữ gián tiếp** = người nhận
- **Tân ngữ trực tiếp** = vật được trao
- Cả hai đều có thể làm chủ ngữ trong passive!

## TỔNG KẾT UNIT 8:

### 🎯 SƠ ĐỒ QUYẾT ĐỊNH KHI NÀO DÙNG PASSIVE:

```
PASSIVE VOICE - Khi nào dùng? 🤔

Không biết ai làm? → YES ✓
Không quan trọng ai làm? → YES ✓  
Muốn trang trọng/khách quan? → YES ✓
Muốn nhấn mạnh kết quả? → YES ✓
Tình huống thông thường? → ACTIVE
```

### 📊 BẢNG THAM KHẢO NHANH:

| Loại | Công thức | Ví dụ |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Basic Passive** | be + V3 | *is made, was built* |
| **Impersonal** | It is said that... | *It is believed that he's honest* |
| **Causative** | have/get + object + V3 | *had my car repaired* |
| **Double Object** | 2 cách passive | *John was given a book / A book was given to John* |

### 📚 CÂU HỎI TỰ KIỂM TRA:
1. "My car was stolen" vs "Someone stole my car" - Khi nào dùng?
2. "I cut my hair" vs "I had my hair cut" - Sự khác biệt?
3. "It is said that" vs "He is said to" - Cái nào trang trọng hơn?

*Tuyệt vời! Chúng ta vừa làm chủ "nghệ thuật lật ngược" với Passive Voice. Tiếp theo, hãy khám phá -ING vs TO-INFINITIVE - cuộc "đối đầu" kinh điển nhất trong ngữ pháp!*

## Renovating My Dream House

Last year, an old Victorian mansion **was purchased** by my family after years of saving. The house **had been abandoned** for nearly a decade, and extensive renovations **were needed** immediately. We **were told** by neighbors that the previous owners **had been forced** to leave suddenly, though the exact reasons **were never explained**. **It is believed that** the house was built in 1890 by a wealthy merchant, and **it is said to have been** one of the finest homes in the area. Now, after months of work, our dream home **is being transformed** into reality. We've **had** most of the major repairs **completed**, though some rooms **are still being renovated**. The experience has taught me that having a house restored **is** both exciting and exhausting.

The initial inspection **was conducted** by a professional surveyor who **was recommended** to us by friends. His report **was delivered** within a week, and we **were shocked** by what **was discovered**. The roof **had been damaged** by years of neglect, and water **had been leaking** into the attic. The original hardwood floors **were covered** with layers of old carpet that **hadn't been removed** in decades. Asbestos **was found** in the basement, which meant it **had to be removed** by specialists. The electrical wiring **was considered** dangerous and **needed to be replaced** immediately. We **were informed** that all the plumbing **would have to be modernized**. The survey results **were presented** to us **with** detailed photographs, and every problem **was explained** carefully.

Once the purchase **was finalized**, we **had** a team of architects **draw up** renovation plans. The original blueprints **were obtained** from the city archives, which helped us understand how the house **had been designed**. Several contractors **were interviewed** before the right team **was chosen**. Their references **were checked** thoroughly, and previous clients **were contacted** for feedback. A detailed contract **was drawn up** by our lawyer, and every aspect of the work **was specified** clearly. The budget **was set** at $150,000, though we **were warned** that unexpected costs might arise. **It is generally accepted that** renovation projects often exceed their initial budgets, and we **were advised** to prepare for this possibility.

The actual work began in spring when the weather **was considered** ideal for construction. First, we **had** the dangerous asbestos **removed** by certified professionals. This work **had to be completed** before any other renovations **could be started**. Next, we **had** new electrical wiring **installed** throughout the house. The old system **was replaced** **with** modern, energy-efficient components. All the work **was inspected** by city officials to ensure it met current codes. We **got** the plumber **to update** all the pipes, and new bathrooms **were fitted** on each floor. The original fireplaces **were restored** by a specialist who **is known to be** an expert in Victorian architecture.

The kitchen renovation **was** the most dramatic transformation. The old kitchen **had been modernized** poorly in the 1970s, and those updates **had to be removed** completely. We **had** custom cabinets **built** by a local craftsman whose work **is thought to be** the finest in the region. New appliances **were delivered** and **installed** last month. The kitchen island **was constructed** from reclaimed wood that **was sourced** from an old barn. Original tiles **were discovered** under the linoleum and **were carefully restored**. The pantry **was converted** into a breakfast nook where morning coffee **can be enjoyed** while sunlight streams through the restored windows.

Unfortunately, we've also **had** some belongings **damaged** during the renovation process. A valuable painting **was accidentally knocked** off the wall by workers, though it **has since been repaired** by a professional restorer. We **had** our antique furniture **moved** to storage after a chair **was scratched** during the construction. Some of grandmother's china **was broken** when a shelf collapsed, which **couldn't be prevented** despite our precautions. These incidents **are said to be** common during major renovations, but they **were** still disappointing. Insurance claims **were filed** for the significant damages, and most costs **were covered** by our policy.

The garden renovation **is being handled** by a landscape designer who **was highly recommended** by the previous renovators of the house. Dead trees **have been removed**, and new ones **are being planted** this week. The old greenhouse **is being rebuilt** using the original plans that **were found** in the attic. A new irrigation system **has been installed**, and the lawn **has been reseeded**. Stone pathways **are being laid** to connect different areas of the garden. We **are having** a gazebo **constructed** where outdoor meals **can be enjoyed** during summer. The fence **was repaired** last week, and the gates **were repainted** in the original Victorian colors.

Modern technology **has been integrated** thoughtfully throughout the house. Smart home systems **were installed** to control heating, lighting, and security. Solar panels **are going to be mounted** on the roof next month to reduce energy costs. High-speed internet cables **were run** through the walls during the electrical work. Security cameras **have been positioned** around the property, and the footage **can be accessed** remotely. **It is estimated that** these upgrades will increase the house's value significantly. However, care **was taken** to ensure that modern additions **weren't allowed** to compromise the historic character.

The restoration of original features **has been given** special attention. The ornate ceiling moldings **were cleaned** and **repaired** by specialists. Original stained glass windows **were removed**, **restored** off-site, and then **reinstalled**. The hardwood floors **were refinished** after years of wear **had been addressed**. Built-in bookcases **were stripped** of old paint and **refinished** to show the natural wood grain. The main staircase **was reinforced** while its carved banister **was preserved**. Every effort **has been made** to maintain the house's authentic Victorian character. **It is believed** by historians that few houses of this era **have been preserved** so carefully.

Local history **has been researched** extensively during our renovation. Old photographs **were discovered** showing the house in its original glory. Former residents **were interviewed** about their memories of the property. **It is said that** famous authors and politicians **were entertained** here in the early 1900s. A hidden room **was discovered** behind a bookcase, where valuable documents **had been stored**. These papers **are being examined** by historians who believe they may be significant. As the project nears completion, lessons **have been learned** about the renovation process. Patience **is required** when dealing with unexpected discoveries and delays. Looking back, this renovation journey **has been** both challenging and rewarding. Our dream house **is being created** through the combined efforts of many skilled professionals. While we **have had** unexpected problems **arise**, solutions **have always been found**. This house, which **was nearly demolished**, **has been given** new life, and we **are honored** to be its caretakers.

## Grammar Features Highlighted:

**Basic Passive:** All tenses demonstrated (*was purchased*, *had been abandoned*, *were needed*, *is being transformed*, *will be installed*, *has been integrated*).

**Continuous Passive:** Present and past continuous forms (*is being handled*, *are being planted*, *was being prepared*).

**Perfect Passive:** Present and past perfect forms (*have been learned*, *had been damaged*, *has been given*).

**Modal Passive:** Various modals with passive (*must be documented*, *should be documented*, *can be enjoyed*, *couldn't be prevented*).

**Impersonal Passive:** Both structures shown (*It is believed that*, *It is said to have been*, *is thought to be*, *is known to be*).

**Causative:** Have/get structures (*had the asbestos removed*, *got the plumber to update*, *having a house restored*, *had our furniture moved*).

**Double Object Passive:** Both options demonstrated (*were told by neighbors*, *was delivered to us*, *has been given special attention*).

**By vs With:** Correct usage (*was conducted by a surveyor*, *were presented with photographs*, *was replaced with modern components*).

**Special Features:**
- Passive without "by" when agent is obvious or unimportant
- Get passive for informal tone (*got the plumber to update*)
- Have + object + past participle for arrangements and unfortunate events
- Impersonal passive for general beliefs and reports

**Common Errors Avoided:**
- No unnecessary "by someone"
- Correct use of "being" in continuous passive
- Proper past participle forms throughout
- No passive with intransitive verbs
- Clear distinction between doing something yourself vs having it done
