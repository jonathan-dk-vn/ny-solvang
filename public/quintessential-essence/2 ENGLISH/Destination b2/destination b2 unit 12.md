# UNIT 12: RELATIVE CLAUSES - NGHỆ THUẬT NỐI CÂU

### 🎯 Tại sao Relative Clauses làm câu nói "sang chảnh"?

So sánh: "I met a man. The man was very tall" vs "I met a man **who was very tall**". Relative Clauses giúp câu nói trở nên mượt mà, tiết kiệm và chuyên nghiệp hơn!

---

## 12.1. DEFINING RELATIVE CLAUSES - MỆH ĐỀ QUAN HỆ XÁC ĐỊNH

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Làm sao phân biệt "cái nào" trong đám đông?

**Bản chất:** Defining relative clauses giúp **xác định cụ thể** người/vật nào chúng ta đang nói đến. **Không có chúng, câu sẽ không rõ nghĩa!**

### 📝 CÁC ĐẠI TỪ QUAN HỆ:

| Đại từ | Dùng cho | Chức năng | Ví dụ |
|--------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **which** | Vật, động vật | Chủ ngữ/Tân ngữ | *The film **which** was on TV* |
| **who** | Người | Chủ ngữ | *The man **who** is tall* |
| **whom** | Người | Tân ngữ (trang trọng) | *The man **whom** we saw* |
| **whose** | Sở hữu | Sở hữu | *The girl **whose** bag is red* |
| **when** | Thời gian | Trạng từ | *The day **when** we met* |
| **where** | Nơi chốn | Trạng từ | *The place **where** I was born* |
| **why** | Lý do | Trạng từ | *The reason **why** he left* |

### 🎯 VÍ DỤ CHI TIẾT:

#### WHICH (cho vật):
- *Did you see the film **which** was on TV last night?*
- **Phân tích:** Bộ phim NÀO? → Bộ phim đã chiếu trên TV!

#### WHO (cho người):
- *Tom Davies, **who** is appearing in concert, is with me.*
- **Phân tích:** Tom Davies NÀO? → Tom Davies đang biểu diễn!

#### WHOSE (sở hữu):
- *My next guest is John Travolta, **whose** career goes back to the 70s.*
- **Phân tích:** Sự nghiệp của John Travolta!

#### WHERE (có thể thay thế):
- *The theatre **where** I first acted...* (= in which I first acted)
- *The theatre **which** I first acted **in**...* (giới từ cuối câu)

### 🔥 ĐẶC ĐIỂM QUAN TRỌNG:

#### 1. Không có dấu phẩy:
- ✅ *This is the DVD which I told you about.*
- ❌ *This is the DVD, which I told you about.*

#### 2. Có thể bỏ đại từ quan hệ khi làm tân ngữ:
- *This is the DVD **(which)** I told you about.*
- *The man **(whom)** we saw yesterday...*

#### 3. Không dùng THAT trong non-defining:
- ✅ *The book which is on the table...* (defining)
- ❌ *My book, that is red, ...* (non-defining)

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Thêm chủ ngữ thừa:**
   - ❌ *The man who he is tall...*
   - ✅ *The man who is tall...*

2. **Nhầm lẫn WHOM:**
   - ❌ *The man whom helped me...*
   - ✅ *The man who helped me.* (WHO làm chủ ngữ)
   - ✅ *The man whom I helped.* (WHOM làm tân ngữ)

## 12.2. NON-DEFINING RELATIVE CLAUSES - MỆNH ĐỀ QUAN HỆ KHÔNG XÁC ĐỊNH

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Khi nào thông tin chỉ "bổ sung thêm"?

**Bản chất:** Non-defining relative clauses chỉ **bổ sung thông tin** - bỏ đi vẫn hiểu câu!

### 🎯 ĐẶC ĐIỂM:

#### 1. Có dấu phẩy:
- *Ray Watson, **who starred in Bandits**, is considering a new film.*

#### 2. Không thể bỏ đại từ quan hệ:
- ❌ *Megamonsters, was filmed in New York, is disappointing.*
- ✅ *Megamonsters, **which** was filmed in New York, is disappointing.*

#### 3. Không dùng THAT:
- ❌ *My car, that is red, is expensive.*
- ✅ *My car, **which** is red, is expensive.*

### 🔥 "WHICH" ĐẶC BIỆT - Thay thế cả câu:
- *We got tickets for the concert, **which** was very lucky.*
- **Phân tích:** "which" = việc chúng ta có được vé

### 🔄 SO SÁNH DEFINING vs NON-DEFINING:

| Defining | Non-defining |
|----------|--------------|
| **Xác định** người/vật cụ thể | **Bổ sung** thông tin |
| **Không** có dấu phẩy | **Có** dấu phẩy |
| **Có thể** bỏ đại từ quan hệ | **Không thể** bỏ |
| **Có thể** dùng THAT | **Không** dùng THAT |
| *Students **who work hard** succeed.* | *My brother, **who lives in London**, is a doctor.* |

## 12.3. PARTICIPLES - PHÂN TỪ

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Cách "rút gọn" relative clauses?

**Bản chất:** Participles giúp **rút gọn câu**, tránh lặp lại và tạo sự mượt mà.

### 📝 CÁC LOẠI PARTICIPLES:

#### 1. Present Participle (V-ing):
- **Thay thế WHO/WHICH + be + V-ing:**
  - *The person **who is directing** the film* = *The person **directing** the film*

#### 2. Past Participle (V3):
- **Thay thế WHO/WHICH + be + V3:**
  - *The person **who is chosen** for the part* = *The person **chosen** for the part*

#### 3. Perfect Participle (having + V3):
- **Thay thế WHO/WHICH + have/has + V3:**
  - *After I **had finished** homework* = *After **having finished** homework*

### 🎯 CÁC CÁCH DÙNG:

#### 1. Sau giới từ và liên từ:
- *By **appearing** in that advert, he damaged his career.*
- *After **playing** video games, I was tired.*

#### 2. Giải thích lý do:
- ***Being** quite good looking, Ralph decided to model.*
- ***Having seen** the film before, I knew the ending.*

#### 3. Hành động đồng thời:
- ***Waiting** for the show, I felt nervous.*

#### 4. Thay thế relative clauses:
- *Imagine being the person **directing** a film!*
- *The person **chosen** will be contacted.*

#### 5. Hành động liên tiếp:
- ***Having finished** homework, I went to cinema.*

#### 6. Thể bị động khác:
- ***Made** to wait, the actor got annoyed.*

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Sai chủ ngữ:**
   - ❌ ***Watching** TV, a news report came on.*
   - ✅ ***Watching** TV, I saw a news report.*

2. **Nhầm lẫn thời gian:**
   - ❌ *Having finished homework, I watch TV.* (sai thì)
   - ✅ *Having finished homework, I watched TV.*

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **Present Participle:** Hành động chủ động, đồng thời
- **Past Participle:** Hành động bị động
- **Perfect Participle:** Hành động trước một hành động khác
- **Chủ ngữ phải giống nhau!**

```
RELATIVE CLAUSES - Chọn loại nào? 🤔

Cần xác định cụ thể? → DEFINING (không dấu phẩy)
Chỉ bổ sung thông tin? → NON-DEFINING (có dấu phẩy)
Muốn rút gọn câu? → PARTICIPLES (-ing/V3/having V3)

Người (tân ngữ)? → WHO/WHOM
Vật? → WHICH
Sở hữu? → WHOSE
```

### 📊 BẢNG THAM KHẢO NHANH:

| Loại | Đặc điểm | Ví dụ |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Defining** | Không dấu phẩy, xác định | *The man who helped me* |
| **Non-defining** | Có dấu phẩy, bổ sung | *My father, who is 60, ...* |
| **Present Participle** | V-ing, chủ động | *The girl singing* |
| **Past Participle** | V3, bị động | *The book written by...* |
| **Perfect Participle** | Having V3, trước | *Having finished, I left* |

## The People and Places That Define Me

Our identities are not formed in a vacuum; they are mosaics, pieced together from the people **who love us** and the places **where we feel at home**. When I reflect on my life, I see a collection of faces and landscapes that have shaped the person **I am today**. These are not just abstract memories; they are the defining elements, the anchors in my story.

My hometown is a place **that will always be a part of me**. I grew up in a small house **which my parents bought** just after they got married. I can still recall the exact day **when we moved in**. The park **where I played** as a child, however, holds the most significant memories. This park, **which is now a designated historic landmark**, was my sanctuary. It was the place for adventures with friends, the people **with whom I shared** my childhood secrets. The specific oak tree **under which we used to sit** still stands there today. We eventually left that town, **which was a difficult transition for a teenager**. That move was a moment **that taught me about change and resilience**.

Beyond places, certain individuals have left an indelible mark on my life. There was my high school English teacher, Mr. Harrison, **who was the first person to encourage my writing**. The feedback **(that) he gave me** was invaluable. Then there is my best friend, Anna, **whose unwavering support has guided me through my toughest times**. Anna, **who is now a successful lawyer in Chicago**, remains the person **I can call at any hour**. It is these people, the ones **who see the best in us**, that truly define our journey. I've learned that a true friend is someone **who believes in you even when you've stopped believing in yourself**.

As I've grown older, I've learned to describe these memories in a more fluid style using participles. Instead of saying "the teacher who inspired me," I might think of him as "the teacher **inspiring me to write**." I can picture Anna, **laughing loudly at a story I’m telling**, her joy being infectious. The advice **given by Mr. Harrison** still echoes in my mind. **Being a naturally shy person**, I found it hard to make friends after we moved. However, **encouraged by my parents**, I joined the school's drama club. **Having decided to take that risk**, I discovered a passion for acting and met a whole new group of creative people. It's crucial to remember that the subject of the participle must match the main clause. It would be incorrect to say, "*Having moved to a new town, my confidence was low*." The correct structure is, "**Having moved to a new town, I felt my confidence was low**."

In conclusion, the people and places of our past are more than just stories. The town **where I grew up** and the teacher **who mentored me** are integral threads in the fabric of my being. My best friend Anna, **whose loyalty is a gift**, continues to be a guiding light. Looking back, I realize how these specific, defining connections have built my world. **Surrounded by such positive influences**, I have been incredibly fortunate. These are the people and places **that make me who I am**.

## Grammar Features Highlighted:

**Defining Relative Clauses:**
* Used to provide essential information with `who`, `which`, `that`, `where`, `when`, and `whom`.
* **Examples:** *the people **who love us**; the place **where we feel at home**; a small house **which my parents bought**; the day **when we moved in**; the person **I am today**.*
* **Pronoun Omission:** Demonstrates omitting the relative pronoun when it's the object (*The feedback **(that) he gave me**...*).
* **No Commas:** Correctly used without commas.
* **Whom:** Correctly used as the object of a preposition (*the people **with whom I shared**...*).

**Non-Defining Relative Clauses:**
* Used to provide extra information, correctly set off by commas.
* **Examples:** *This park, **which is now a designated historic landmark**, was my sanctuary; Anna, **who is now a successful lawyer in Chicago**, remains my best friend.*
* **`Which` referring to a whole clause:** *We eventually left that town, **which was a difficult transition**...*
* **`Whose`:** Used in a non-defining context (*Anna, **whose unwavering support has guided me**...*).

**Participles (Clause Reduction):**
* **Present Participle (-ing):** Used for active actions and providing reasons (*...the teacher **inspiring me to write**; Anna, **laughing loudly...**; **Being a naturally shy person**...*).
* **Past Participle (V3):** Used for passive actions (*The advice **given by Mr. Harrison**...; **Encouraged by my parents**...*).
* **Perfect Participle (Having + V3):** Used for an action that happened before the main action (* **Having decided to take that risk**, I discovered...*).
* **Common Errors Avoided:** The essay explicitly corrects a potential dangling participle error, ensuring the subject of the participle and the main clause match.

**Comparison and Cohesion:**
* The essay naturally contrasts defining clauses (the specific house/teacher/day) with non-defining clauses (extra information about the park/Anna).
* The final paragraphs transition smoothly into using participles as a more sophisticated way to convey the same ideas presented earlier with full relative clauses, demonstrating their function as a "shortcut."
