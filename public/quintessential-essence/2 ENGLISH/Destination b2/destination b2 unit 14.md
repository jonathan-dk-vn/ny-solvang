## 14.1. POSSESSIVE 'S AND S' - SỞ HỮU CÁCH

### 🤔 Mở đầu: "John's car" hay "the car of John"?

**Bản chất:** Sở hữu cách thể hiện **mối quan hệ sở hữu** một cách ngắn gọn và tự nhiên.

### 📝 QUY TẮC CƠ BẢN:

#### 1. Danh từ số ít + 's:
- *That's the **manager's** car.*
- *My **sister's** book is interesting.*

#### 2. Danh từ số nhiều kết thúc bằng -s + ':
- *The **workers'** pay was increased.*
- *My **parents'** house is big.*

#### 3. Danh từ số nhiều không kết thúc bằng -s + 's:
- *The **children's** toys are everywhere.*
- ***Women's** rights are important.*

### 🕐 BIỂU HIỆN THỜI GIAN:
- *After an **hour's** wait, I saw the manager.*
- *I need a **week's** holiday.*
- *It's a **ten minutes'** walk.*

### 🎯 KHI NÀO DÙNG 'S/S':

#### ✅ Với người và động vật:
- *The **company's** finances* (công ty = nhóm người)
- *The **cat's** paw*

#### ❌ Với đồ vật (dùng OF):
- ❌ *The computer's back*
- ✅ *The back **of** the computer*

### 🔥 TRƯỜNG HỢP ĐẶC BIỆT:

#### Tên riêng kết thúc bằng -s:
- *Charles**'** computer* hoặc *Charles**'s** computer* (cả hai đều đúng)

#### Tên số nhiều:
- *The **Smiths'** business* (gia đình Smith)

#### Cụm từ dài:
- *Tom, Dick and **Harry's** office* ('s ở cuối cùng)
- *The **President of France's** visit* ('s ở cuối cụm)

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Đặt sai vị trí 's:**
   - ❌ *Tom's and Dick's and Harry's office*
   - ✅ *Tom, Dick and Harry's office*

2. **Dùng với đồ vật:**
   - ❌ *The car's engine*
   - ✅ *The engine of the car*

3. **Nhầm lẫn số ít/số nhiều:**
   - ❌ *The boy's are playing.* (boys đang chơi)
   - ✅ *The boys are playing.*
   - ✅ *The boy's toys are here.* (đồ chơi của cậu bé)

## 14.2. POSSESSIVE DETERMINERS & PRONOUNS

### 🤔 Mở đầu: "My book" vs "Mine" - Khi nào dùng cái nào?

### 📊 BẢNG SO SÁNH:

| Possessive Determiners | Possessive Pronouns |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| **my** | **mine** |
| **your** | **yours** |
| **his** | **his** |
| **her** | **hers** |
| **its** | *- (không có)* |
| **our** | **ours** |
| **their** | **theirs** |

### 🎯 CÁCH DÙNG:

#### Possessive Determiners (+ noun):
- *I'm excited about **my** new job.*
- *Is this **your** book?*

#### Possessive Pronouns (thay thế noun):
- *My job is great. How about **yours**?*
- *This book is **mine**.*

#### Sau "of":
- *She's a colleague **of mine**.*
- *That friend **of yours** is funny.*

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Nhầm lẫn determiner vs pronoun:**
   - ❌ *Mine book is here.*
   - ✅ *My book is here.*
   - ❌ *How about your?*
   - ✅ *How about yours?*

2. **Thêm apostrophe vào pronoun:**
   - ❌ *The book is their's.*
   - ✅ *The book is theirs.*

3. **Nhầm ITS vs IT'S:**
   - ✅ *The company increased **its** profits.* (sở hữu)
   - ✅ ***It's** a great company.* (it is)

## 14.3. INVERSIONS - ĐẢO NGỮ

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Tại sao đảo ngữ làm câu nói "sang chảnh"?

**Bản chất:** Inversions tạo **hiệu ứng nhấn mạnh** và thể hiện **trình độ cao** - như "đũa phép" của tiếng Anh!

---

## 14.4. INVERSIONS WITH NEGATIVE ADVERBIALS

### 🎯 QUY TẮC CHUNG:
**Negative adverbial + auxiliary + subject + main verb**

### 📝 CÁC TỪ/CỤM TỪ THƯỜNG DÙNG:

| Adverbial | Ví dụ |
|-----------|-------|
| **Never** | ***Never** have I worked so hard.* |
| **Rarely/Seldom** | ***Rarely** have I seen such talent.* |
| **No sooner...than** | ***No sooner** had he arrived **than** it started raining.* |
| **Hardly...when** | ***Hardly** had I sat down **when** the phone rang.* |
| **Not only...but also** | ***Not only** is he smart, **but** he's also funny.* |
| **Under no circumstances** | ***Under no circumstances** should you give up.* |
| **At no time/point** | ***At no time** was I told about this.* |
| **Little** | ***Little** did I know how hard it would be.* |
| **Not until** | ***Not until** midnight did he return home.* |
| **Only** | ***Only** when I met her did I understand love.* |

### 🔥 PHÂN TÍCH CHI TIẾT:

#### LITTLE (với động từ suy nghĩ):
- ***Little** did I realise = I didn't realise at all*
- Dùng với: know, realise, suspect, understand

#### NOT UNTIL/ONLY - Chú ý động từ nào đảo:
- ✅ ***Not until** I finished homework **was I allowed** to go out.*
- ❌ ***Not until** I **was finished** homework I allowed to go out.*

### 🎯 VÍ DỤ THỰC TẾ:
- **Bình thường:** *I have never worked so hard in my life.*
- **Đảo ngữ:** ***Never** have I worked so hard in my life.*

- **Bình thường:** *He had no sooner arrived than it rained.*
- **Đảo ngữ:** ***No sooner** had he arrived than it rained.*

## 14.5. OTHER INVERSIONS - ĐẢOTNGỮ KHÁC

### 🎯 CÁC LOẠI KHÁC:

#### 1. Short answers với SO/NEITHER/NOR:
- *"I'm a plumber!" "Really? **So am I**!"*
- *"I don't like coffee." "**Neither do I**" / "**Nor do I**."*

#### 2. Sau AS/SO/SUCH:
- *The manager was nervous, **as were** the staff.*
- ***Such** a hot day **was it** that no one worked.*

#### 3. Conditional inversions:
- ***Were** I you, I would be careful.* (= If I were you...)
- ***Had** I known, I would have come.* (= If I had known...)

### 🔄 SO vs NEITHER/NOR:

| Tình huống | Đáp lại |
|------------|---------|
| **Câu khẳng định** | **So + aux + I** |
| *"I like coffee."* | *"**So do I**."* |
| **Câu phủ định** | **Neither/Nor + aux + I** |
| *"I don't like coffee."* | *"**Neither do I**."* |

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Nhầm lẫn so và neither:**
   - ❌ *"I don't like it." "So do I."*
   - ✅ *"I don't like it." "Neither do I."*

2. **Sai trật tự với NOT UNTIL:**
   - ❌ ***Not until** he **had** finished **was** allowed to leave.*
   - ✅ ***Not until** he had finished **was he allowed** to leave.*

3. **Quên đảo ngữ:**
   - ❌ ***Never** I have seen such beauty.*
   - ✅ ***Never** have I seen such beauty.*

## TỔNG KẾT UNIT 14:

### 🎯 SƠ ĐỒ QUYẾT ĐỊNH POSSESSIVES:

```
POSSESSIVES - Chọn cách nào? 👑

Người/động vật + đồ vật? → 'S/'
Đồ vật + đồ vật? → OF
Trước noun? → MY/YOUR/HIS...
Thay thế noun? → MINE/YOURS/HIS...
Tên kết thúc -s? → ' hoặc 's
Số nhiều kết thúc -s? → '
```

### 🎯 SƠ ĐỒ QUYẾT ĐỊNH INVERSIONS:

```
INVERSIONS - Khi nào đảo? ⚡

Negative adverbial đầu câu? → ĐẢO NGỮ
Never/Rarely/Little...? → aux + subject + verb
Short answer đồng ý? → So/Neither + aux + S
Muốn trang trọng/nhấn mạnh? → INVERSIONS
Câu bình thường? → Trật tự thường
```

### 📊 BẢNG THAM KHẢO NHANH:

| Loại | Công thức | Ví dụ |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Possessive 's** | Noun + 's | *John's car* |
| **Possessive Determiner** | my/your... + noun | *my book* |
| **Possessive Pronoun** | mine/yours... | *The book is mine* |
| **Negative Inversion** | Neg.adv + aux + S + V | *Never have I...* |
| **So/Neither** | So/Neither + aux + S | *So do I* |

## The Unwritten Rules: My Family's Traditions

Every family has **its** own unique culture, a set of traditions that are rarely written down but are understood by all. In my family, these traditions are the anchors of our year, shaping our gatherings and strengthening our bonds. **Never** have I seen these customs broken; they are the unwritten rules that define us. From the specific roles each person plays to the timing of every event, these moments are not just habits but cherished ceremonies.

The ownership of tasks and memories is clearly, if informally, defined. My **grandmother's** kitchen is the heart of all our celebrations. Her recipes are a closely guarded secret, and the results are a testament to a **lifetime's** practice. During the holidays, my **parents'** main job is decoration. The festive atmosphere they create is magical for the **children's** enjoyment. The most significant event is my **Aunt Jane and Uncle Tom's** anniversary party. The responsibility for the music is always my **brother's**, while the task of organizing the guest list is **mine**. The house itself belongs to them, but on that day, the joy is **ours** to share. A friend of **yours** might find our structured approach amusing, but for us, it’s a beautifully coordinated dance.

Our traditions are governed by rules that are communicated with emphatic gravity. **Under no circumstances** is anyone late for the annual family photo. **Not only** is this picture a historical record, **but it is also** a source of great pride for my grandfather. As children, **little** did we understand the importance of these moments. We just knew the rules. **No sooner** had the photo been taken **than** we were allowed to run off and play. I particularly remember the rule about dessert: **not until** everyone had finished the main course **was the cake allowed** to be cut. This structure, inverting the main clause, perfectly captures the dramatic weight of the moment. **Rarely** have I tasted anything as delicious as my **grandmother's** chocolate cake after a long, patient wait.

These traditions create a powerful sense of unity and shared feeling. My father loves telling old family stories. "**So does my uncle**." My younger cousins, however, don't enjoy sitting still for so long. "**Neither do I**, if I'm being honest," my sister once whispered to me. The bond is palpable. **Such** was the feeling of togetherness that even newcomers felt instantly welcome. Reflecting on it now, I see the past with new eyes. **Were** I to have my own family one day, I would continue these traditions. **Had** I known then how fast time would pass, I would have cherished each moment even more. These aren't just my **family's** traditions; they are a legacy.

In the end, these rituals are more than just repeated actions. They are the language of our family's love. They are the back of the photo album, not the *photo album's back*. They belong to the **Jameses'** history, a story passed down through generations. The traditions are not just **his** or **hers**; they are **theirs**, **ours**, and a precious inheritance for the future. **Seldom** does a family create something so strong out of such simple, repeated acts of love and gathering.

## Grammar Features Highlighted:

**Possessives:**
* **'s and s':** Correctly used with singular (`grandmother's`), plural (`parents'`), and irregular plural (`children's`) nouns.
* **Time Expressions:** `a lifetime's practice`.
* **Object vs. Person:** The essay avoids the error of using `'s` for an inanimate object by correctly phrasing it (*the back of the photo album*).
* **Special Cases:** Shows possession with a long phrase (`Aunt Jane and Uncle Tom's`), a family name (`the Jameses' history`).
* **Possessive Determiners vs. Pronouns:** Demonstrates the difference with `my brother's` (role), `mine` (role), `ours` (joy), `yours` (friend of), `his`, `hers`, and `theirs`.
* **Its vs. It's:** Correctly uses `its` for possession (*Every family has its own...*) and `it is` (*it's a beautifully coordinated dance*).

**Inversions:**
* **Negative Adverbials:** The essay uses a wide range of inversions for emphasis.
    * `Never`: *Never have I seen...*
    * `Under no circumstances`: *Under no circumstances is anyone late...*
    * `Not only...but also`: *Not only is this picture..., but it is also...*
    * `Little`: *Little did we understand...*
    * `No sooner...than`: *No sooner had the photo been taken than...*
    * `Not until`: *Not until everyone had finished... was the cake allowed...* (correctly inverting the main clause).
    * `Rarely`/`Seldom`: *Rarely have I tasted...*; *Seldom does a family create...*
* **Other Inversions:**
    * `So/Neither/Nor`: *So does my uncle*; *Neither do I*.
    * `Conditional Inversions`: *Were I to have...*; *Had I known...*
    * `Such...that`: *Such was the feeling of togetherness that...*
* **Common Errors Avoided:** The essay correctly inverts the auxiliary and subject after negative adverbials and correctly inverts only the main clause for `Not until`.
