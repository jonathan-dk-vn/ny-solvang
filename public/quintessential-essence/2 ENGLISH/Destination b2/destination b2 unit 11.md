# UNIT 11: REPORTED SPEECH - NGHỆ THUẬT "KỂ LẠI"

### 🎯 Tại sao Reported Speech quan trọng?

Hàng ngày, chúng ta đều "kể lại": "John said he was busy", "She told me she would come", "He asked if I could help"... Reported Speech chính là cách chúng ta truyền đạt thông tin một cách chính xác và tự nhiên!

---

## 11.1. REPORTED SPEECH: THAY ĐỔI THÌ VÀ MODAL

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Tại sao "I am tired" thành "He said he **was** tired"?

**Bản chất:** Khi kể lại, chúng ta **"lùi thời gian"** một bước - như xem lại một bộ phim cũ!

### 📊 BẢNG CHUYỂN ĐỔI THÌ:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech | Ví dụ |
|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Present Simple** | **Past Simple** | *"I **need** help" → He said he **needed** help.* |
| **Present Continuous** | **Past Continuous** | *"I**'m working**" → She said she **was working**.* |
| **Present Perfect** | **Past Perfect** | *"I**'ve finished**" → He said he **had finished**.* |
| **Present Perfect Continuous** | **Past Perfect Continuous** | *"I**'ve been waiting**" → She said she **had been waiting**.* |
| **Past Simple** | **Past Perfect** | *"I **went** there" → He said he **had gone** there.* |
| **Past Continuous** | **Past Perfect Continuous** | *"I **was sleeping**" → She said she **had been sleeping**.* |

### 📊 BẢNG CHUYỂN ĐỔI MODAL:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech | Ví dụ |
|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| **will** | **would** | *"I**'ll help**" → He said he **would help**.* |
| **can** | **could** | *"I **can swim**" → She said she **could swim**.* |
| **may** | **might** | *"I **may come**" → He said he **might come**.* |
| **must/have to** | **had to** | *"I **must go**" → She said she **had to go**.* |

### 🔥 MODAL KHÔNG ĐỔI:
- **would, could, might, should, ought to** giữ nguyên!
- *"I **would** like coffee" → He said he **would** like coffee.*

### ⚠️ NGOẠI LỄ - KHÔNG CẦN ĐỔI THÌ:

#### 1. Sự thật khoa học:
- *"Water **boils** at 100°C" → He said water **boils** at 100°C.*

#### 2. Vẫn còn đúng ở hiện tại:
- *"I **live** in Paris" → She said she **lives** in Paris.* (nếu vẫn sống ở Paris)

## 11.2. REPORTED SPEECH: THAY ĐỔI ĐẠI TỪ & TỪ HẠN ĐỊNH

### 🤔 Mở đầu: "My car" thành "his car" hay "her car"?

**Bản chất:** Thay đổi **quan điểm kể** - từ góc nhìn người nói chuyển sang góc nhìn người kể lại.

### 📊 BẢNG CHUYỂN ĐỔI ĐẠI TỪ:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech | Ví dụ |
|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| **I/me/my/mine** | **he/him/his** (hoặc she/her/hers) | *"**My** car" → He said **his** car* |
| **you/your/yours** | **I/me/my/mine** (hoặc they/them/their) | *"**Your** book" → He told me **my** book* |
| **this/these** | **that/those** | *"**This** is nice" → She said **that** was nice* |
| **we/us/our/ours** | **they/them/their/theirs** | *"**We** are ready" → They said **they** were ready* |

### 🎯 VÍ DỤ CHI TIẾT:
- **Direct:** *Tim said, "I've lost **my** credit card."*
- **Reported:** *Tim said he had lost **his** credit card.*

- **Direct:** *"I love **this** sweater," said Tim.*
- **Reported:** *Tim said he loved **that** sweater.*

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **I/my → he/his hoặc she/her** (tùy giới tính)
- **this/these → that/those** (xa hơn về thời gian)

---

## 11.3. REPORTED SPEECH: THAY ĐỔI THỜI GIAN & ĐỊA ĐIỂM

### 🤔 Mở đầu: "Yesterday" thành gì trong reported speech?

**Bản chất:** Thay đổi **tham chiếu thời gian** - từ thời điểm người nói sang thời điểm người kể lại.

### 📊 BẢNG CHUYỂN ĐỔI THỜI GIAN:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech | Ví dụ |
|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| **now/at the moment** | **then/at that moment** | *"I'm busy **now**" → He said he was busy **then**.* |
| **today** | **that day** | *"I'm leaving **today**" → She said she was leaving **that day**.* |
| **tomorrow** | **the next/following day** | *"I'll call **tomorrow**" → He said he would call **the next day**.* |
| **yesterday** | **the day before/the previous day** | *"I went **yesterday**" → She said she had gone **the day before**.* |
| **last week/month/year** | **the week/month/year before** | *"I traveled **last month**" → He said he had traveled **the month before**.* |
| **next week/month/year** | **the following week/month/year** | *"I'll start **next week**" → She said she would start **the following week**.* |
| **ago** | **before/previously** | *"I met him **2 days ago**" → She said she had met him **2 days before**.* |

### 🏠 BẢNG CHUYỂN ĐỔI ĐỊA ĐIỂM:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech | Ví dụ |
|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| **here** | **there** | *"I live **here**" → He said he lived **there**.* |
| **this place** | **that place** | *"I like **this place**" → She said she liked **that place**.* |

### 🔥 NGOẠI LỆ - KHÔNG CẦN THAY ĐỔI:
Khi thông tin vẫn còn đúng tại thời điểm kể lại:

- **Direct:** *"I'm going shopping **tomorrow**," said Tim to Ben.*
- **Reported (ngay lập tức):** *Ben immediately called and said, "Tim said he's going shopping **tomorrow**."*

### 🎯 VÍ DỤ TỔNG HỢP:
- **Direct:** *"I went shopping **here yesterday**," said Tim.*
- **Reported:** *Tim said he had gone shopping **there the day before**.*

## 11.4. REPORTED QUESTIONS - CÂU HỎI GIÁN TIẾP

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Làm sao "kể lại" một câu hỏi?

**Bản chất:** Câu hỏi gián tiếp **mất dấu hỏi chấm** và **trở về trật tự bình thường**!

### 📝 QUY TẮC CHUNG:
- **Không có dấu hỏi chấm**
- **Trật tự: S + V** (không phải trật tự câu hỏi)
- **Áp dụng tất cả quy tắc thay đổi thì, đại từ, thời gian**

### 🎯 YES/NO QUESTIONS → IF/WHETHER:

| Direct Question | Reported Question |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| *"**Can** you help me?" he asked.* | *He asked **if/whether** I could help him.* |
| *"**Are** you coming?" she asked.* | *She asked **if/whether** I was coming.* |
| *"**Did** you see the film?" he asked.* | *He asked **if/whether** I had seen the film.* |

### 🎯 WH-QUESTIONS → Giữ nguyên từ hỏi:

| Direct Question | Reported Question |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| *"**Where** do you live?" she asked.* | *She asked **where** I lived.* |
| *"**What** time is it?" he asked.* | *He asked **what** time it was.* |
| *"**Why** are you late?" she asked.* | *She asked **why** I was late.* |

### 🔥 SO SÁNH TRẬT TỪ:

| Loại | Trật tự từ | Ví dụ |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Direct Question** | Aux + S + V + ? | *"Where **do you** live?"* |
| **Reported Question** | S + V (no ?) | *He asked where **I lived**.* |

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Giữ nguyên trật tự câu hỏi:**
   - ❌ *He asked where did I live.*
   - ✅ *He asked where I lived.*

2. **Thêm dấu hỏi chấm:**
   - ❌ *She asked if I was coming?*
   - ✅ *She asked if I was coming.*

3. **Quên IF/WHETHER với Yes/No questions:**
   - ❌ *He asked could I help.*
   - ✅ *He asked if I could help.*

## 11.5. REPORTING VERBS - ĐỘNG TỪ TƯỜNG THUẬT

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Không chỉ có "said" và "told"!

**Bản chất:** Các động từ khác nhau thể hiện **cách thức, thái độ** khác nhau khi nói.

### 📊 CÁC NHÓM REPORTING VERBS:

#### Nhóm 1: verb + that clause
- **said, told, explained, mentioned, replied:**
  - *She **said (that)** she was tired.*
  - *He **told me (that)** he would come.*

#### Nhóm 2: verb + to-infinitive
- **agreed, offered, promised, refused, threatened:**
  - *She **agreed to help** me.*
  - *He **refused to come**.*

#### Nhóm 3: verb + object + to-infinitive
- **advised, asked, begged, invited, ordered, reminded, warned:**
  - *She **advised me to study** harder.*
  - *He **asked her to wait**.*

#### Nhóm 4: verb + -ing
- **admitted, denied, suggested:**
  - *He **admitted stealing** the money.*
  - *She **suggested going** to the cinema.*

#### Nhóm 5: verb + preposition + -ing
- **accused...of, apologised for, insisted on:**
  - *He **accused me of lying**.*
  - *She **apologised for being** late.*

### 🎯 VÍ DỤ THỰC TẾ:

#### DENY (3 cách):
1. **deny + noun:** *Katie denied the accusation.*
2. **deny + that clause:** *Katie denied (that) she was a shoplifter.*
3. **deny + -ing:** *Katie denied stealing the chocolate.*

#### SUGGEST (2 cách):
1. **suggest + -ing:** *He suggested going to the cinema.*
2. **suggest + that clause:** *He suggested (that) we go to the cinema.*

### 🔄 SO SÁNH SAY vs TELL:

| SAY | TELL |
|-----|------|
| **say (that)...** | **tell + person + (that)...** |
| *He **said** he was tired.* | *He **told me** he was tired.* |
| Không cần đối tượng | Cần đối tượng (ai được nói) |

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Nhầm lẫn SAY vs TELL:**
   - ❌ *He said me he was busy.*
   - ✅ *He told me he was busy.*
   - ✅ *He said he was busy.*

2. **Dùng sai cấu trúc với SUGGEST:**
   - ❌ *He suggested me to go.*
   - ✅ *He suggested that I go.*
   - ✅ *He suggested going.*

3. **Quên giới từ:**
   - ❌ *He accused me lying.*
   - ✅ *He accused me of lying.*

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **SAY:** Nói gì (không cần nói với ai)
- **TELL:** Nói với ai (cần có đối tượng)
- **Mỗi reporting verb có cấu trúc riêng** - cần học thuộc!

## TỔNG KẾT UNIT 11:

### 🎯 SƠ ĐỒ CHUYỂN ĐỔI REPORTED SPEECH:

```
REPORTED SPEECH - Các bước chuyển đổi 📝

1. Lùi thì (Present → Past, Past → Past Perfect...)
2. Đổi đại từ (I → he/she, you → I/they...)
3. Đổi thời gian (yesterday → the day before...)
4. Đổi địa điểm (here → there...)
5. Câu hỏi: bỏ dấu ?, trật tự S+V, thêm if/whether
```

### 📊 BẢNG THAM KHẢO NHANH:

| Loại | Thay đổi | Ví dụ |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Thì** | Lùi 1 bước | *am → was, will → would* |
| **Đại từ** | Đổi góc nhìn | *I → he/she, my → his/her* |
| **Thời gian** | Lùi về quá khứ | *today → that day, tomorrow → the next day* |
| **Câu hỏi** | Mất dấu ?, S+V | *"Are you OK?" → asked if I was OK* |

### 📚 CÂU HỎI TỰ KIỂM TRA:
1. "I will come tomorrow" → Reported speech như thế nào?
2. "Are you ready?" → Reported question ra sao?
3. "He said me" vs "He told me" - Cái nào đúng?

## News from an Old Friend: A Reported Conversation

Yesterday was one of those surprisingly wonderful days. While grabbing coffee, I ran into my old college friend, Mark, who I hadn't seen in years. We decided to sit down and catch up, and the conversation flowed for hours. He **told me that** he had been doing very well and **said that** he was happy to see me. Later, when I recounted the meeting to my sister, I realized how much of our communication relies on the art of reporting what others have said.

Mark started by giving me a summary of his life. He **said he was working** as a software developer for a tech startup downtown. He explained that although he had originally studied history, he **had decided** to change careers a few years before. When I asked about his living situation, he **told me he had bought** his apartment just last year, and he proudly **added that was** his greatest personal achievement. His plans for the future sounded exciting; he mentioned he **would travel** to South Korea the following month for a conference. He also said he **could already speak** some basic Korean, which really impressed me. I remembered Mark always being a quick learner. He even brought up a universal truth, reminding me that the Earth **revolves** around the Sun, a fact that obviously required no tense change in reporting.

Of course, I was curious and had many questions for him. First, I **asked where he was living**. He smiled and told me he had moved to the Northwood district. I then **asked if he still played** the guitar, remembering our old college band. He laughed and replied that he rarely had time anymore. He then turned the questions on me, asking **what I had been up to**. He also **asked whether I had seen** our old friend, Sarah, recently. He explained that he **had to call** her soon because he had promised to help her with a project. His tone was so genuine, and it was clear he was still the same caring person.

The conversation was more than just an exchange of facts; it was full of offers, advice, and suggestions, which required a variety of reporting verbs to recount accurately. At one point, I mentioned a problem I was having with my website, and he immediately **offered to take a look** at it for me. He **advised me to learn** some basic coding, saying it was an essential skill in today's world. When talking about his recent vacation, he **admitted spending** way too much money. He **suggested going** on a trip together sometime, which I thought was a fantastic idea. He even **apologized for not staying** in touch more often. This part of the conversation really highlighted the difference between `say` and `tell`; he **said** many interesting things, but he **told me** specifically that I should be more confident in my career.

As we were about to leave, he **invited me to a party** he was hosting the following weekend at his place. He **promised it would be** fun and **reminded me to bring** a friend if I wanted to. I, in turn, **agreed to be there**. Reflecting on our conversation now, it's amazing how much ground we covered. He **said he would call** me later that week to finalize the details. It was a simple conversation, but recounting it makes me appreciate the nuances of language—the subtle shifts in time, perspective, and tone that allow us to share our experiences with others.

## Grammar Features Highlighted:

**Tense and Modal Changes:**
* **Present Continuous → Past Continuous:** *...he **was working** as a software developer...*
* **Past Simple → Past Perfect:** *...he **had decided** to change careers...* / *...he **had bought** his apartment...*
* **Will → Would:** *...he **would travel** to South Korea...*
* **Can → Could:** *...he **could already speak** some basic Korean...*
* **Must/Have to → Had to:** *...he **had to call** her soon...*
* **Exception (Fact):** The essay includes an example of a universal truth that does not change tense (*...the Earth **revolves** around the Sun*).

**Pronoun, Time, and Place Changes:**
* **Pronoun Shift:** `my` → `his` (*...that was **his** greatest personal achievement.*)
* **Determiner Shift:** `this` → `that` (*...**that** was his greatest...*)
* **Time Shift:** `next month` → `the following month`, `last year` → `the year before` (implied in the past perfect sentence), `now` → `then` (implied in the question).

**Reported Questions:**
* **WH-Question:** Correctly uses statement word order (*I asked **where he was living**.*), avoiding the common error of keeping the inverted question form.
* **Yes/No Question:** Correctly uses `if/whether` (*I asked **if he still played** the guitar*; *...asked **whether I had seen**...*).
* **No Question Mark:** All reported questions correctly end with a period.

**Reporting Verbs:**
* **say vs. tell:** The difference is explicitly shown and used correctly (*He **told me** he had been doing well*; *He **said** he was happy*; *he **told me** specifically...*).
* **verb + that clause:** `told`, `said`, `explained`, `added`, `mentioned`, `reminded`.
* **verb + to-infinitive:** `offered to take`, `agreed to be`.
* **verb + object + to-infinitive:** `advised me to learn`, `invited me to a party`, `reminded me to bring`.
* **verb + -ing:** `admitted spending`, `suggested going`.
* **verb + preposition + -ing:** `apologized for not staying`.
* **Common Errors Avoided:** The essay correctly uses patterns like `suggested going` instead of the incorrect `suggested me to go`.
