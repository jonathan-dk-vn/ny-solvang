# Phrasal Verbs

## 1. Movement and Travel
### 1.1. General Movement
- **catch up (with)**: reach the same point/level as
- **keep up with**: stay at the same point/level as
- **turn around**: go back in the opposite direction
- **make for**: go in the direction of
- **go back (to)**: return (to)
- **come back (from)**: return (from)
- **run away (from)**: escape by running
- **go away**: leave a place/sb

### 1.2. Transportation
- **pick up**: stop in a vehicle to give someone a lift
- **pull in**: stop by the side of the road in a car
- **run over**: hit with a car
- **drop off**: let someone get out of a vehicle
- **take off**: leave the ground (for planes)
- **get in(to)**: enter a car
- **get on(to)**: enter a bus/train/etc
- **get off**: leave a bus/train/etc
- **get out (of)**: leave a car/building/room/etc

### 1.3. Travel and Journeys
- **check in**: register at a hotel or an airport
- **check out**: leave a hotel
- **see off**: go to a train station, etc, to see someone leave
- **get back**: return from a place
- **set out/off**: start a journey
- **go away**: go on holiday

## 2. Actions and Activities
### 2.1. Starting and Continuing
- **carry on**: continue
- **get round to**: start (after planning to do sth for a long time)
- **join in**: participate, take part
- **take up**: start (a hobby, sport, etc)
- **keep on**: continue doing sth
- **get on with**: continue doing
- **go on**: continue happening or doing sth

### 2.2. Stopping or Delaying
- **put off**: delay, postpone
- **pull out**: stop being involved in an activity
- **turn off**: stop a machine working
- **break down**: stop working (for a machine, etc)
- **call off**: cancel
- **shut up**: stop talking, stop making a noise

### 2.3. Performing and Producing
- **carry out**: perform an experiment, etc
- **give off**: produce sth such as heat or a smell
- **plug in**: connect to the electricity supply
- **put on**: hold; perform (a show, play, etc)
- **bring out**: produce and start to sell a new product
- **draw up**: create (plans, etc)

### 2.4. Changing or Transforming
- **turn into**: change into sth different
- **come off**: succeed
- **narrow down**: reduce the number of possibilities
- **cross out**: draw a line through sth written
- **do up**: repair, paint or improve; button/zip up a piece of clothing
- **build up**: increase

### 2.5. Interacting and Engaging
- **look out**: be careful
- **watch out**: be careful
- **go in for**: enter (a competition, etc); like
- **put up with**: tolerate
- **go off**: stop liking
- **take to**: start (as a habit); begin to like
- **show off**: try to attract people's attention and make them admire you
- **try out**: experiment with
- **line up**: get/put into lines
- **send off**: make a player leave a game (eg, football)
- **point out**: tell sb important information

### 2.6. Communication and Connection
- **put through**: connect by phone
- **come up with**: think of (an idea, a plan, etc)
- **come on**: develop or make progress; be quicker
- **call back**: ring again on the phone
- **hang up**: put the receiver down to end a phone call
- **speak up**: talk more loudly so sb can hear you
- **read out**: say sth out loud which you are reading
- **hang on**: wait

### 2.7. Removal or Reduction
- **cut off**: stop the supply of sth; disconnect (phone, electricity, etc); completely remove by cutting
- **leave out**: not include
- **do away with**: get rid of
- **throw away**: get rid of, discard; put sth in a rubbish bin
- **tear down**: destroy or remove (for buildings, statues, etc)
- **pull off**: break by pulling
- **rip up**: tear into pieces
- **rub out**: remove with a rubber
- **take away**: remove
- **take down**: remove (from a high place)
- **keep out**: prevent from entering

### 2.8. Physical Actions
- **pick up**: lift something from the floor, a table, etc
- **put down**: stop holding
- **put up**: put something on a wall (eg, a picture)
- **hang up**: put clothes in a wardrobe, etc
- **put away**: return sth to where it belongs
- **put back**: return something to where it was
- **take back**: return sth to the place it came from
- **sit down**: (start to) sit
- **stand up**: (start to) stand
- **lie down**: start lying (on a bed, etc)

## 3. Information and Understanding
### 3.1. Discovering and Learning
- **find out**: discover information, etc
- **work out**: find the solution to a problem, etc
- **look up**: try to find information in a book, etc
- **catch on**: understand
- **dawn on**: if something dawns on you, you realize it for the first time
- **sort out**: solve a problem
- **add up**: find the total of

### 3.2. Communicating and Expressing
- **bring up**: start discussing a subject
- **put forward**: suggest
- **get at**: try to express
- **set out**: explain, describe or arrange sth in a clear and detailed way
- **go into**: deal with sth in detail
- **make up**: invent an explanation, excuse, etc; create a story, poem, etc
- **write down**: write information on a piece of paper

### 3.3. Observing and Reviewing
- **flick through**: turn and look at the pages of a magazine, etc quickly
- **turn over**: turn a page so that the other side is towards you
- **look round**: examine (a place)
- **go over**: repeat or think about again in order to understand completely

### 3.4. Broadcasting and Publishing
- **come on**: start to be broadcast
- **come out**: be published

### 3.5. Clarifying and Distributing
- **make out**: pretend that something is true; see, hear or understand sb or sth with difficulty
- **see through**: recognise that sth is not true and not be tricked by it
- **stand out**: be easy to see because of being different
- **hand out**: give things to people in a group
- **fill in**: add information in the spaces on a document
- **ask after**: ask for news about
- **share out**: give a part of sth to a group of people
- **print out**: make a paper copy of sth on a computer

## 4. Relationships
### 4.1. Positive Interactions
- **make up**: become friends again after an argument
- **get on (with)**: have a good relationship (with)
- **get along (with)**: have a good relationship (with)
- **look up to**: admire and respect
- **stand up for**: support in an argument or fight
- **take after**: look or behave like an older relative

### 4.2. Negative Interactions
- **fall out (with)**: have an argument with and stop being friends
- **put down**: criticize, make someone feel stupid
- **pick on**: keep treating someone badly or unfairly
- **look down on**: think that you are better than

### 4.3. Life Stages and Changes
- **bring up**: look after a child until he or she becomes an adult
- **grow up**: become older (for children)
- **settle down**: become calm after being upset, etc; stay in one place or get married and live quietly
- **pass away**: die
- **move in**: start living in a new house, etc
- **go out with**: be the boyfriend/girlfriend of
- **split up**: end a relationship

### 4.4. Emotional Responses
- **fall for**: fall in love with; believe (a lie/trick/etc)
- **take aback**: surprise (usually in passive voice)
- **let down**: disappoint
- **fall for**: believe that a trick or joke is true
- **grow on**: if sth grows on you, you start to like it more
- **cheer up**: become/make happier
- **calm down**: become/make calmer

## 5. Challenges and Difficulties
### 5.1. Conflict and Escape
- **back down**: stop demanding sth, stop saying that you will do sth
- **break out**: escape (from prison); start suddenly (for a war, fire, etc)
- **make off**: escape
- **get away with**: escape punishment for
- **chase after**: follow sb/sth quickly in order to catch them
- **break in(to)**: enter illegally

### 5.2. Investigation and Resolution
- **look into**: investigate
- **come forward**: offer help or information
- **take down**: write down what someone says
- **deal with**: handle, cope with
- **face up to**: accept sth and try to deal with it
- **see through (to)**: continue (or help to continue) to the end of sth unpleasant or difficult

### 5.3. Authority and Law
- **hand in**: give to a person in authority
- **bring in**: introduce a new law or system
- **let off**: give little or no punishment; make a bomb, etc explode
- **hold up**: rob while threatening violence; delay
- **blow up**: explode

### 5.4. Health Challenges
- **come down with**: start to suffer from a minor illness
- **pass out**: suddenly become unconscious
- **come round/to**: become conscious
- **pull through**: survive (a serious illness, etc)
- **bring on**: cause (an illness, etc)
- **get (sb) down**: make sb feel sad or lose hope
- **give up**: stop doing sth you do regularly
- **fall down**: trip and fall

### 5.5. Demands and Responsibilities
- **call for**: require; need; demand
- **take on**: start to employ; accept (work or responsibility)
- **set to**: start doing sth in a determined or enthusiastic way
- **hurry up**: do sth more quickly

## 6. Health and Lifestyle
### 6.1. Health Management
- **cut down (on)**: do less of (smoking, etc); reduce an amount of
- **get over**: recover from (an illness, etc)
- **wear off**: stop being effective (for a drug, etc)
- **come down with**: start to suffer from a minor illness
- **feel up to**: feel well enough to do
- **put on**: gain (weight)
- **look after**: take care of

### 6.2. Lifestyle Choices
- **put off**: make sb not want to do or not like sth
- **put down**: kill (a sick/old animal)
- **drop in (on)**: visit unexpectedly
- **get on for**: be almost a particular time, number, age, etc
- **dress up**: put on fancy or unusual clothes
- **take off**: remove (a piece of clothing)
- **try on**: put on (a piece of clothing) to see how it looks and whether it fits
- **grow out of**: develop from; become too big for
- **have on**: wear (a piece of clothing)
- **get up**: leave your bed
- **stay up**: go to bed late
- **wake up**: stop being asleep
- **eat out**: eat at a restaurant
- **wash up**: wash plates, cups, cutlery, etc

## 7. Miscellaneous
### 7.1. Chance and Opportunity
- **run into**: meet by chance
- **come across**: find sth or meet sb by chance
- **come by**: get sth, especially sth that is hard to get
- **turn up**: appear unexpectedly or without making a firm arrangement

### 7.2. Resource Management
- **run out (of)**: not have any left
- **get through**: use all of; finish
- **do without**: live without (sth you can't afford)
- **put by**: save an amount of money for the future
- **save up (for)**: save money little by little (for a specific purpose)
- **live on**: use as a source of money
- **fill up**: make sth completely full
- **give back**: return sth you have taken/borrowed
- **pay back**: return money (to sb)

### 7.3. Persistence and Outcome
- **go on**: continue happening or doing sth; do sth after doing sth else
- **turn out**: develop in a particular way or have a particular result
- **sail through**: do something or deal with something very easily
- **think over**: consider
- **make up for**: provide sth good, so that sth bad seems less important
- **get by**: manage to survive (financially)

### 7.4. Reputation and Recognition
- **go down (as)**: be remembered for having done something
- **stand for**: represent (for abbreviations and symbols); put up with
- **name after**: give sb or sth the same name as sb or sth else
- **take off**: become successful or popular very fast
- **catch on**: become popular or fashionable

### 7.5. Visiting and Interaction
- **go/come round**: go/come to sb's house to visit them
- **pop in(to)**: visit quickly or for a short time
- **show (a)round**: take sb on a tour of a place

### 7.6. Change and Adaptation
- **come (a)round (to)**: be persuaded to change your mind (about)
- **clear up**: become brighter and better (for weather); tidy
- **set in**: start and be likely to continue for a while (for rain, winter, an economic depression, etc)
- **die down**: become less noisy, powerful or active
- **slow down**: decrease speed
- **speed up**: increase speed

### 7.7. Handling and Processing
- **put out**: make something stop burning
- **tear up**: rip into pieces
- **wear out**: become old and unusable
- **make out**: write all the necessary information on a cheque, etc
- **drown out**: prevent a sound from being heard by making a louder noise
- **hand down**: give something valuable to your children or grandchildren, usually when you die
- **go off**: no longer be fresh
- **go out**: stop burning

### 7.8. Business and Organization
- **set up**: start (a business, organization, etc)
- **take over**: take control of (a business, etc)
- **close down**: stop operating (for companies)
- **stand in for**: do sb's job for them while they are not available
- **see to**: deal with
- **set out**: start working on sth in order to achieve an aim
- **turn down**: not accept (an offer, request, etc); lower the volume of
- **turn up**: increase the volume of
- **turn on**: start a machine working

### 7.9. Miscellaneous Actions
- **drop out (of)**: leave school, etc before you have finished a course
- **put down to**: suggest that sth is the result of
- **give away**: give free of charge; reveal sth you are trying to hide
- **come into**: inherit
- **bank on**: depend on sth happening
- **count on**: rely on; trust
- **back out**: decide not to do sth you agreed to do
- **log on(to)**: connect to the Internet/a website
- **log off**: disconnect from the Internet/a website
