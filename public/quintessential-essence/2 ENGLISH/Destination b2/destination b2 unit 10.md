## 10.1. QUESTIONS - CÂU HỎI CƠ BẢN

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Tại sao "Where you are from?" nghe sai sai?

**Bản chất:** Câu hỏi trong tiếng Anh có **trật tự từ đặc biệt** - không giống tiếng Việt!

### 📝 CÁC LOẠI CÂU HỎI:

#### 1. Với BE làm động từ chính:
| Thì | Câu hỏi |
|-----|---------|
| **Hiện tại** | *Are you tired?* |
| **Quá khứ** | *Were they at home?* |
| **Tiếp diễn** | *Is he working?* |

#### 2. Với HAVE làm động từ chính:
| Cách dùng | Câu hỏi |
|-----------|---------|
| **Have got** | *Have you got any money?* |
| **Have (khác)** | *Do you have enough time?* |

#### 3. Với modal verbs:
- *Should I wait?*
- *Could you help me?*
- *Will Jay be here soon?*

#### 4. Với động từ thường:
| Thì | Câu hỏi |
|-----|---------|
| **Hiện tại** | *Do you like coffee?* |
| **Quá khứ** | *Did they come yesterday?* |

### 🔥 WH-QUESTIONS:

| Từ hỏi | Hỏi về | Ví dụ |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Who** | Người | *Who is taking the rubbish out?* |
| **Whose** | Sở hữu | *Whose book is this?* |
| **Whom** | Người (tân ngữ) | *To whom did you speak?* |
| **What** | Vật/Việc | *What is the weather like?* |
| **Which** | Lựa chọn | *Which do you want?* |
| **Where** | Nơi chốn | *Where did you go?* |
| **When** | Thời gian | *When is Terry starting work?* |
| **Why** | Lý do | *Why did they leave?* |
| **How** | Cách thức | *How do you spell 'environment'?* |

### 🔥 PHÂN BIỆT CHỦ NGỮ vs TÂN NGỮ:

#### WHO làm chủ ngữ (không dùng DO):
- *Who **called** you?* (Ai đã gọi bạn?)

#### WHO làm tân ngữ (dùng DO):
- *Who **did** you **call**?* (Bạn đã gọi ai?)

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Quên đảo ngữ:**
   - ❌ *Where you are from?*
   - ✅ *Where are you from?*

2. **Dùng sai trợ động từ:**
   - ❌ *Did you went there?*
   - ✅ *Did you go there?*

3. **Nhầm lẫn với "mean":**
   - ❌ *What means 'environment'?*
   - ✅ *What does 'environment' mean?*

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **Trật tự:** WH-word + Auxiliary + Subject + Main Verb
- **Với BE/HAVE/Modal:** Không cần trợ động từ
- **WHO chủ ngữ:** Không cần DO

## 10.2. QUESTION TAGS - "ĐUÔI" CÂU HỎI

### 🤔 Mở đầu: "You're from Vietnam, aren't you?" - Tại sao có "aren't you"?

**Bản chất:** Question tags như **"phần đuôi"** - giúp câu nói thân thiện hơn và kiểm tra thông tin.

### 📝 QUY TẮC CHUNG:
**Câu khẳng định + question tag phủ định**
**Câu phủ định + question tag khẳng định**

### 🎯 CÁC TRƯỜNG HỢP:

#### 1. Với BE:
- *You are Canadian, **aren't you**?*
- *She isn't beautiful, **is she**?*

#### 2. Với auxiliary verbs:
- *You haven't lost my CD, **have you**?*
- *We are having lesson early, **aren't we**?*

#### 3. Với modal verbs:
- *People should recycle, **shouldn't they**?*
- *There will be many people, **won't there**?*

#### 4. Với HAVE (sở hữu):
- *Tom has a car, **hasn't he** / **doesn't he**?* (cả hai đều đúng)

#### 5. Với động từ thường:
- *You play guitar, **don't you**?*
- *Frank lives in Germany, **doesn't he**?*

### 🔥 TRƯỜNG HỢP ĐẶC BIỆT:

#### I am:
- *I'm the best student, **aren't I**?* (không phải "am not I")
- *I'm not very tall, **am I**?*

#### Let's:
- *Let's get a DVD tonight, **shall we**?*

#### Imperatives:
- *Pass me that book, **will you**?*
- *Don't forget tonight, **will you**?*

#### Everyone/Someone/No one:
- *Everyone's going, **aren't they**?* (dùng "they")
- *Someone's been here, **haven't they**?*
- *No one wants to go, **do they**?* (từ phủ định → tag khẳng định)

#### There:
- *There's no point calling, **is there**?* (lặp lại "there")

### 🔊 NGỮ ĐIỆU:

| Mục đích | Ngữ điệu | Ví dụ |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Đồng ý** | Xuống ↘ | *It's hot, isn't it?* ↘ |
| **Kiểm tra** | Lên ↗ | *You're Spanish, aren't you?* ↗ |

### 🇺🇸🇬🇧 KHÁC BIỆT MỸ - ANH:
- **Mỹ:** *They've got money, **don't they**?*
- **Anh:** *They've got money, **haven't they**?*

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Sai quy tắc khẳng định/phủ định:**
   - ❌ *You are tired, are you?*
   - ✅ *You are tired, aren't you?*

2. **Nhầm lẫn với câu có từ phủ định:**
   - ❌ *No one came, didn't they?*
   - ✅ *No one came, did they?*

## 10.3. INDIRECT QUESTIONS - CÂU HỎI GIÁN TIẾP

### 🤔 Mở đầu: Làm sao hỏi một cách "lịch sự"?

**Bản chất:** Cách hỏi **lịch sự, trang trọng** hơn - đặc biệt khi nói với người lạ hoặc trong môi trường formal.

### 📝 CẤU TRÚC:
**Introductory phrase + clause (trật tự bình thường)**

### 🎯 CÁC CỤM MỞ ĐẦU:

| Cụm mở đầu | Ví dụ |
|------------|-------|
| **Could you tell me...?** | *Could you tell me **what time it is**?* |
| **Could you let me know...?** | *Could you let me know **when it starts**?* |
| **Do you know...?** | *Do you know **who that woman is**?* |
| **I wonder if you could tell me...** | *I wonder if you could tell me **how much this costs**.* |
| **I would like to know...** | *I would like to know **what your company will do**.* |

### 🔥 ĐIỂM QUAN TRỌNG:

#### 1. Trật tự từ bình thường:
- ❌ *Could you tell me where is the library?*
- ✅ *Could you tell me where **the library is**?*

#### 2. Yes/No questions → IF/WHETHER:
- **Direct:** *Is he coming?*
- **Indirect:** *I wonder **if/whether** he is coming.*

#### 3. Dấu hỏi chấm:
- *Could you tell me where the library is**?*** (có dấu hỏi)
- *I wonder where the library is**.*** (không có dấu hỏi)

### 🔄 SO SÁNH DIRECT vs INDIRECT:

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| *Where is the bank?* | *Could you tell me where the bank is?* |
| *What time does it start?* | *Do you know what time it starts?* |
| *Are you married?* | *I wonder if you are married.* |

### ⚠️ LỖI SAI THƯỜNG GẶP:

1. **Giữ nguyên trật tự câu hỏi:**
   - ❌ *I wonder when does the train leave.*
   - ✅ *I wonder when the train leaves.*

2. **Quên IF/WHETHER với Yes/No questions:**
   - ❌ *Could you tell me are you coming?*
   - ✅ *Could you tell me if you are coming?*

### 💡 MẸO GHI NHỚ:
- **Indirect questions:** Trật tự như câu khẳng định
- **Yes/No → IF/WHETHER**
- **Lịch sự hơn direct questions**

### 🎯 SƠ ĐỒ QUYẾT ĐỊNH LOẠI CÂU HỎI:

```
QUESTIONS - Chọn loại nào? 🤔

Hỏi thông tin trực tiếp? → DIRECT QUESTIONS
Muốn lịch sự/trang trọng? → INDIRECT QUESTIONS
Kiểm tra thông tin/tạo không khí thân thiện? → QUESTION TAGS
Hỏi về người (chủ ngữ)? → WHO (không dùng do)
Hỏi về người (tân ngữ)? → WHO + DO
```

### 📊 BẢNG THAM KHẢO NHANH:

| Loại | Cấu trúc | Ví dụ |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Direct** | WH + Aux + S + V | *Where do you live?* |
| **Indirect** | Phrase + S + V | *Could you tell me where you live?* |
| **Question Tags** | Statement + opposite tag | *You're tired, aren't you?* |
| **Yes/No Indirect** | Phrase + if/whether + S + V | *I wonder if you're coming.* |

### 📚 CÂU HỎI TỰ KIỂM TRA:
1. "Where you live?" vs "Where do you live?" - Cái nào đúng?
2. "You can swim, can you?" vs "You can swim, can't you?" - Cái nào đúng?
3. "Could you tell me where is the bank?" vs "Could you tell me where the bank is?" - Cái nào đúng?

## The Art of Connection: Making New Friends

Making new friends as an adult can often feel like a challenge, can't it? The process fundamentally relies on the art of conversation, and the very soul of conversation is the question. **How do you start a conversation with a stranger? What do you say?** The way we ask questions determines whether a conversation blossoms or fades away. From direct inquiries to gentle, rapport-building tags, understanding the different forms of questions is essential for anyone looking to expand their social circle.

When you first meet someone, direct questions are the most common tool. The key is to remember the basic structure: (WH-word) + Auxiliary + Subject + Verb. It’s a simple formula that prevents common mistakes, such as asking "*Where you are from?*" instead of the correct "**Where are you from?**" Basic questions like "**Are you enjoying the party?**" or "**Have you got any plans for the weekend?**" are great icebreakers. To delve deeper, one might ask, "**What does your typical workday look like?**" It's important to use the right auxiliary verb; you wouldn't ask "*Did you went to the concert last night?*", but rather "**Did you go...?**" A particularly interesting feature of English questions is the difference between asking about the subject and the object. You might ask, "**Who introduced you to this group?**" where 'Who' is the subject and no 'do' is needed. But if you wanted to know about their companion, you would ask, "**Who did you come with?**" where 'Who' is the object. Mastering these direct forms is the first step toward confident communication.

Once a conversation is flowing, question tags become invaluable for creating a friendly atmosphere. The golden rule is simple: a positive statement is followed by a negative tag, and vice versa. Saying, "**You work in marketing, don't you?**" with a rising intonation is a great way to check information you're unsure about. On the other hand, stating, "**It's a beautiful day, isn't it?**" with a falling intonation invites agreement and builds connection. English has some fascinating special cases. If you feel you're dominating the conversation, you might say, "**I'm talking a lot, aren't I?**" using the unique tag for "I am." To make a friendly suggestion, you could say, "**Let's exchange numbers, shall we?**" Even a simple request can be softened: "**Don't forget to add me on social media, will you?**" When observing a group, you might remark, "**Everyone seems to be having a good time, don't they?**" It's crucial to remember that words like 'no one' are negative, so they take a positive tag; for instance, "**No one knows each other here, do they?**" This avoids the common error of using a double negative.

While direct questions are useful, there are times when a softer, more polite approach is needed, especially when asking for personal information or speaking to someone in a more formal setting. This is where indirect questions excel. Instead of asking bluntly, "**What time does the event end?**," you could ask, "**Do you know what time the event ends?**" The essential trick is that after the introductory phrase, the word order returns to that of a normal statement. A frequent mistake is to keep the question's inverted order, like "*Could you tell me where is the nearest cafe?*" The correct, polite form is "**Could you tell me where the nearest cafe is?**" For Yes/No questions, 'if' or 'whether' is used. Rather than asking "**Are you free for coffee next week?**," a more considerate inquiry would be, "**I was wondering if you are free for coffee next week.**" This gentle approach shows respect and makes the other person feel more comfortable.

Ultimately, the ability to ask questions effectively is a social superpower. It allows us to learn about others, show interest, and build the foundations of a lasting friendship. Moving seamlessly between a direct "**What kind of music do you like?**" and a softer "**I'd like to know what your favorite bands are**" demonstrates social intelligence. Using tags helps transform a simple exchange into a shared experience. The art of making new friends isn't about having all the right answers; it's about asking all the right questions, isn't it?

## Grammar Features Highlighted:

**Direct Questions:**
* **WH-Questions:** Correctly formed with auxiliary verbs (*How do you start...?*, *What do you say?*, *Where are you from?*, *Who did you come with?*).
* **Yes/No Questions:** With 'be' (*Are you enjoying...?*), 'have got' (*Have you got...?*), and 'do' (*Did you go...?*).
* **Subject vs. Object Questions:** Demonstrates the difference between `Who introduced you...?` (subject) and `Who did you come with?` (object).
* **Common Errors Avoided:** The essay explicitly corrects common mistakes like "*Where you are from?*" and "*Did you went...?*" and shows the correct way to ask what a word means.

**Question Tags:**
* **Basic Rule:** Demonstrates positive statement + negative tag (*It's a beautiful day, isn't it?*) and the reverse.
* **Special Cases:**
    * `I am` -> `aren't I?` (*I'm talking a lot, aren't I?*)
    * `Let's` -> `shall we?` (*Let's exchange numbers, shall we?*)
    * `Imperatives` -> `will you?` (*Don't forget..., will you?*)
    * `Everyone/No one` -> Uses 'they' and shows the correct positive tag for negative words (*Everyone seems..., don't they?*, *No one knows..., do they?*).
* **Intonation:** Mentions the difference between rising (checking) and falling (agreement) intonation.

**Indirect Questions:**
* **Politeness:** Framed as a more polite way to ask questions.
* **Introductory Phrases:** Uses `Do you know...`, `Could you tell me...`, and `I was wondering...`.
* **Normal Word Order:** Correctly uses statement word order after the phrase (*...what time the event ends*, *...where the nearest cafe is*). This directly addresses and corrects the most common error.
* **If/Whether:** Correctly uses 'if' for a Yes/No indirect question (*I was wondering if you are free...*).

**Cohesion and Context:**
* The theme "Making New Friends" provides a natural and logical context for using all types of questions, from initial icebreakers (direct) to building rapport (tags) and asking more sensitive questions (indirect).
